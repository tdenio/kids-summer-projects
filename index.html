01 How the workshop runs

The deal

One kid, one parent

Each project block is one-on-one. Swap kids day to day so you both get time with each, and no project is stuck waiting on one parent.

Solo + together

Each explorer keeps their own Scratch game, design, and code. The movie, treasure hunts, and the final showcase are built as a team.

A menu, not a boot camp

This is a rhythm, not a rulebook. Swap blocks, skip ahead, chase a question that's working. Curiosity wins over the clock.

You earn the lights

Good effort, good attitude, and a clean workshop light up a bulb each day. Light up the week and Friday ends with up to an hour of screen time.

03 The ten-week trail

From first spark to showcase

A suggested arc — three projects run all summer (Arduino, the movie, and Scratch); the rest come in focused sprints. Slide weeks to match your calendar.

WEEK 1

Launch & Explore

Set up the workshops. Make Explorer ID cards & expedition logos, run the first compass treasure hunt, unbox the Arduino kit, brainstorm the movie.

WEEK 2

Design I

Learn SketchUp basics and design the birdhouse. First Arduino lesson. First Scratch project.

WEEK 3

Design II

Design the small cabin and build a cardboard model of it. Storyboard the summer movie.

WEEK 4

Lights & Logic

Arduino LEDs and patterns. Pick a documentary subject in the yard and map the filming spots.

WEEK 5

Documentary I

Shoot footage with the camera and learn a few real shots. Keep the Scratch game going.

WEEK 6

Documentary II

Edit in DaVinci Resolve and finish the documentary cut.

WEEK 7

Movie I

Gather the $20 of approved props and film scenes. Experiment with syncing the Arduino lights to music.

WEEK 8

Movie II

Finish filming, start the edit, and run a lock-picking challenge.

WEEK 9

Final Builds

Assemble the musical Christmas lights, polish the edits, and take a stretch run at Revit if it's clicking.

WEEK 10

The Showcase

Family Film Festival & Maker Fair: premiere the movie and documentary, demo the lights-to-music, present designs and maps, hand out certificates.

04 The project shelf

What's on the bench

Pick what fits the week. The last two cards are blank on purpose — your best ideas go there.

SPINE

Arduino Musical Lights

The summer's headline build — Christmas lights that blink in time with music, taught step by step.

Tools: Tech Toast Academy kit
Tech Toast is a 25-week course — you won't finish it this summer, and that's fine. Aim for roughly 8–10 lessons that carry you to the lights-to-music finale. One kit? Pair up and trade off. Two kits? Build in parallel.
DESIGN

Design Studio

Design a birdhouse and a small cabin for the neighboring lot, then build a cardboard model you can hold.

Tools: SketchUp · cardboard · Kreg project ideas
Use the free SketchUp for Schools in a browser. Revit is real pro architecture software — keep it as a stretch taste, not a goal.
FILM

Yard Documentary

Pick something living in your yard — bugs, birds, a garden, the weather — and tell its story on camera.

Tools: kids' digital camera · DaVinci Resolve
Plan it, shoot it, edit it. In DaVinci, just learn five moves: import, trim, cut, add titles, export. That's a real edit.
FILM

The Summer Movie

A 10-minute movie, due at the showcase. Storyboard it first so everyone knows the shots.

Tools: sketchboard · DaVinci Resolve · props
Up to $20 of props, approved by Mom & Dad. Storyboard before a single scene is filmed.
EXPLORE

Mapmaking & Expeditions

Explorer ID cards, expedition logos, compass treasure hunts, and a hand-drawn map of your own land.

Tools: compass · paper · markers
Perfect fuel for the morning Move block — hide a cache, draw the route, send the other explorer after it.
LAB

Lock Lab

How locks actually work — pins, tension, and patient problem-solving with a practice set.

Tools: practice lock set
House rule: practice locks only — never anyone's real locks or property — and always with a parent.
CODE

Code Lab: Scratch

Build a game or animation by snapping code blocks together. Maybe even animate the movie's title card.

Tools: a web browser
Scratch lives at scratch.mit.edu — free, in the browser, built for ages 8+. Heads up: "scratch.io" isn't the real site.

06 Workshop rules

Ask before you order

Kits, props, and purchases get the green light from Mom & Dad first.

Sharp & powered tools

Anything that cuts, heats, or plugs in is used with a grown-up.

Clean bench, clear head

Every block ends the way it started — workshop reset.

Pitch your ideas

Got a better build? Make the case. Good pitches get added to the shelf.
