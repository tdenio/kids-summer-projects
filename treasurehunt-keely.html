01 Using your compass

Read before you hunt

Hold it flat

Keep the compass level so the needle can spin freely. Tilt it and the needle drags on the case.

Wait for the needle

The red end always points North. Wait for it to stop swinging before you read your bearing.

Walk a bearing

Turn your whole body until the bearing number you need lines up with the direction-of-travel arrow. Then walk. Check every 10–15 steps.

Count your steps

One normal walking step ≈ 2½ feet. So 4 steps ≈ 10 feet, 20 steps ≈ 50 feet. Try to keep a steady stride.

N = 0° NE = 45° E = 90° SE = 135° S = 180° SW = 225° W = 270° NW = 315°
