01 The brief

What you are designing

This is a real cabin on a real lot next door. Treat it like a real project — every decision you make has to fit a person actually living there.

~1,000 sq ft

That’s roughly a 32 × 32 foot square — a little bigger than a two-car garage. Not large, but plenty for one person.

1 Bedroom

At least 120 sq ft. Needs a window and a closet. Where does the morning light come from? That’s usually the best wall for a bedroom window.

1 Bathroom

Minimum 40 sq ft for a toilet, sink, and small shower. Should be accessible from the bedroom without walking through the living area.

Kitchen + Living

The rest of the space — roughly 600–700 sq ft. Open plan works well in small cabins so the space feels bigger.

02 Locate the building site

Mark the four corners

Use your compass and pacing to stake out the cabin footprint on the actual lot. One walking step ≈ 2½ feet. For a 32 × 32 ft cabin you need about 13 steps per side.

Before you start: Pick a clear starting landmark on the lot (a fence post, a tree, a corner stake). Write it below. All four corners are measured from there or from each other in sequence.
